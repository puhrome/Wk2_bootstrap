My Strange Adventures

February, 2014, Buffalo, NY.

I think the air temperature was 10 degrees when I took this. We had gone downtown to get tacos from our friend who was working on a food truck at the time.

The longer I am away from Buffalo and Rochester, the more I realize (and am told) I have that “Midwestern Nice Guy Thing” going on. And I’m ok with that, because I guess it’s who I am.

October, 2014 Seattle, WA

I have been thinking a lot about the whole adage “distance makes the heart grow fonder.”

For a long time, I didn’t write, I didn’t read, I didn’t take pictures, and I didn’t draw anything. I just worked and raced bikes. And then worked some more. And then rode some more.

Within the last year, I started doing those things again. I also started riding bikes for reasons other than racing for the first time in a long time.

And it makes me feel human again.

I look at my time in the saddle differently than I did while I was racing all the time. For a few years, saddle time was training time, though I refused to recognize it as such. Turning the pedals became about some sort of end goal… Time, distance, intervals, races, better results, etc, etc.

Now it’s about mid ride beer stops and going at whatever pace I or we want to. And smiles. And friendship. And riding for the pure joy of it.
